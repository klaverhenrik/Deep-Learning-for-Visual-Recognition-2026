# Peer Show-and-Tell
### Deep Learning for Visual Recognition — Week 12

The session is an open supervised work session. The first 30 minutes
are structured as a lightweight peer show-and-tell.

---

## Format

Groups are paired up. Each pair spends **15 minutes on each group's work**,
for a total of 30 minutes before the open session begins.

You will be assigned a partner group at the start of the session.

---

## How it works

**For each group (15 minutes):**

**Minutes 0–5: Show**
One person from the presenting group walks the other group through
their current results. Show your best result, your confusion matrix
or equivalent, and one figure you are happy with.

You do not need slides. Just share your screen or open your notebook.

**Minutes 5–12: Discuss**
The other group asks questions and gives feedback.

Use these prompts if you are not sure what to say:
- "What does your model get most wrong, and do you understand why?"
- "How does your best result compare to your baseline?"
- "Is there anything in your results table you do not understand yet?"
- "What would make this result more convincing?"

**Minutes 12–15: Takeaway**
The presenting group names one concrete thing they will improve
based on the feedback. Write it down.

Then swap — the other group presents for 15 minutes.

---

## What to bring

- Your current best result (a number)
- Your confusion matrix or equivalent error analysis figure
- One figure you are happy with and want feedback on
- One thing you are unsure about or stuck on

You do not need a polished presentation. This is a conversation, not a demo.

---

## Why this is useful

You have been working on your project for weeks. It is easy to lose
perspective on what is obvious and what needs explanation, what is
a good result and what needs more work.

Explaining your work to someone outside your group forces you to
articulate things you may have been taking for granted. The questions
you cannot answer clearly are the sections of your report that need
more work.

Reading other groups' work also gives you calibration — you will
see what a well-structured results section looks like, and what
a confused one looks like.

---

## After the show-and-tell

Open work session for the remaining time.

- [ ] All figures generated and formatted correctly
- [ ] A draft results section written up (even rough)
- [ ] A clear list of what is still missing before submission
