# Week 13: Final Open Session
### Deep Learning for Visual Recognition — Week 13

**Submission is at the end of this week.**

This is an unstructured open session. There is no new content,
no worksheets, no presentations. Just three hours of supervised
work time with TAs available to answer questions.

---

## How to use this session

Come with a specific task. Groups that arrive without a plan
tend to leave without having accomplished much.

**Suggested priorities, roughly in order:**

1. **Run any missing experiments.**
   If your results table has gaps — ablations you planned but
   did not run, a comparison you wanted to make — do them now.
   After today there is no more supervised GPU time.

2. **Finalise your results section.**
   Every table and figure should be in its final form.
   Check against the Week 11 guide — axis labels, captions,
   consistent metric, bold best result.

3. **Write up your analysis.**
   The discussion of your results is often the hardest part
   to write and the part most students leave too late.
   What did you learn? Where does your model fail? What would
   you try next if you had more time?

4. **Check your code submission.**
   Your code should run from top to bottom without errors
   on a fresh Colab session. Test this before submitting.
   Include a README with instructions for running the code.

5. **Proofread.**
   Read your report out loud. If a sentence does not make sense
   when spoken, it will not make sense when read either.

---

## What TAs can help with today

- Running experiments and interpreting results
- Debugging code that is not working
- Feedback on a specific section of your report
- Choosing between two ways of presenting a result
- Anything else project-related

TAs cannot write your report or run your experiments for you,
but they can help you get unstuck on specific problems.

---

## A note on scope

If you are discovering major gaps in your work today, focus on
what you can do well rather than trying to do everything.

A report that analyses three experiments thoroughly is better
than one that describes ten experiments superficially.
A results section with honest failure analysis is better than
one that only shows successes.

Quality over quantity.
